Humidity and temperature inside the hive during the cold snap of December, 2021.

The measurements are taken at the top board, not inside the hive. The outside temperature went below -12 Â°C on one night, and usually around -7 Â°C the other nights. How and what did the bees do?

We would expect they would cluster inside, and that looks like what happened. The humidity being exhausted from the hive dropped very low as the temperatures dipped.

Note the red circles below:
These show where, periodically, the humidity would spike above 90% for just a few moments, and at the exact same time, the temperature spiked. I interpret that mean the bees on the outside of the cluster exhanged with warm bees from the inside. This took about 15 minutes, during which moisture and heating escaped as the bees unlocked from each other.

Mid-January warming

Note how the internal hive humidity varies continuously through the cold part of January. But as it warms in the second week, the humidity suddenly shoots up to 100% (at the top board). Looks like the bees are no longer needing to cluster! Instead, they're active and are expelling a lot of moisture out the top. ðŸ™‚

Overview: from the Dec '21 cold snap to the end of February.

For the most part, it looks like they're pretty active. We have a few cool nights where it looks like they clustered a bit, but overall, they're staying active. The red box shows the first days where we noticed large numbers out flying. More details in the next tab.

We also noticed that the interal hive temperature has been affected less and less by cold temperatures. This suggests a rapidly growing population, just what we'd hope for at this time of the year. More bees=more ability to thermally regulate the hive.

First warm days in February, 2022

The red boxes show three sunny days we had, and the time where we observed lots of bees out and about, bringing back pollen and such. Note that the internal temperature really spikes, and the humidity is all over the place. This is probably because lots of bee activity and traffic. It doesn't look like any pattern seen during the cold weather.

On the rainy day, the pattern continued. It was still quite warm, so they probably weren't clustering, but they were obviously up to something. After that, the humidity has gone to 100%, except for some brief drops during our recent week of cold temperatures.
